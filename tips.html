3. Tips

✦ essential advice ✦

From mountain trails to city streets — travel smarter in Nepal.

Before you go

  • Visa & passport: Most nationalities can get a visa on arrival at Kathmandu airport (bring USD cash and passport photos). Check Department of Immigration for updates.
  • Travel insurance: Mandatory for trekking — ensure it covers high-altitude evacuation (up to 5500m+). Keep digital + printed copies.
  • Best seasons: October–November (clear skies, festivals) & March–May (rhododendrons bloom). Monsoon (June–Sept) is muddy but green.
  • Vaccinations: Routine + Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended. Consider altitude sickness medication (consult doctor).

🙏 Culture & respect

  • Greetings: “Namaste” with palms together is the warm, respectful hello. Use your right hand for giving/receiving.
  • Temples & stupas: Walk clockwise around Buddhist stupas and Hindu shrines. Remove shoes before entering. Leather items may be restricted.
  • Dress modestly: Especially in rural areas and religious sites. Shoulders & knees covered shows respect.
  • Feet & head: Feet are considered unclean — never point soles at people or deities. The head is sacred; avoid touching someone’s head.

⛑️ Health & altitude smarts

  • Altitude sickness (AMS): Ascend slowly, hydrate like crazy, and know the symptoms (headache, nausea). “Climb high, sleep low.”
  • Water & food: Drink only bottled, boiled, or treated water. Street food? Go where it's busy and freshly cooked — momo steam is your friend.
  • First-aid kit: Carry blister plasters, rehydration salts, and basic meds. Pharmacies in cities are well-stocked.
  • Air quality: Kathmandu can be dusty. Bring a buff or mask, especially in dry season.

💵 Money & daily life

  • Cash is king: ATMs exist in cities, but carry enough Nepalese Rupees (NPR) for remote areas. Many guesthouses don’t take cards.
  • Bargaining: Polite haggling is common in street markets and for taxis (always agree fare beforehand). Fixed-price shops are clear.
  • SIM cards: Ncell or Nepal Telecom — get one at the airport or official stores. You’ll need a passport photo and copy of visa.
  • Power cuts: Less frequent now, but a power bank is essential for treks. Many teahouses charge for charging.

⭐ PRO TIP — NEPALI HOSPITALITY ⭐

Nepalis are incredibly friendly. Learn a few words: “Dhanyabad” (thank you), “Tapailai kasto cha?” (how are you?). Smile, and you'll be welcomed like family.

👉 Always ask permission before photographing people — especially sadhus or during ceremonies. A small donation is appreciated at temples.

🚌 Getting around

  • Taxis & rides: Use Pathao or inDrive apps in Kathmandu/Pokhara — they show fair prices. Otherwise negotiate before you hop in.
  • Tourist buses: Comfortable for intercity travel (KTM–Pokhara, Chitwan). Book a day ahead. Greenline and others offer AC.
  • Domestic flights: Weather dependent — always keep a buffer day before international flight. Mountain views from the plane are unreal!

⬤ Enjoy the Himalayas, respect the culture, and leave only footprints. ⬤
